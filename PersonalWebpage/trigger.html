Overview: Collisions at the LHC occur with and event rate ~40 MHz. However current technologies can only record a fraction of this data. The CMS trigger system selects events of interest from the large number of less interesting 'background' events by looking for specific signatures of interest.

CMS utilises a two-tier trigger system. The first, Level-1 trigger, runs on high-performance custom-built electronics systems to perform a very fast coarse reconstruction of the event. Reducing the data rate from 40 MHz to 100 kHz. The second, High Level Trigger (HLT), comprises of software running on a computer farm. Which given a larger reconstruction time can perform a more detailed reconstruction of the event before making the final decision whether to retain the event.
